02 — The manifesto

Four reasons worth a weekend.

Four scenes. Read each one out loud. If it lands, you're in the right place.
I

Stop consuming.

You've watched the tutorials. Saved the threads. Bookmarked the playlists. You don't need more inputs — you need a deadline.

II

Learn by shipping.

No frameworks. No "intro to AI" slides. You learn the platform by deploying a real product on it — and then doing it again.

III

Rooms beat Discords.

Two chairs over is faster than two replies later. The network forms on its own when builders sit next to each other.

IV

A URL counts. A Figma doesn't.

No mockups. No "would have built." Just a real product, deployed, that you can share. Earned, not described.

04 — Q & A

Quick answers.

Everything you need before you reserve a spot.
Q.01Do I need to know how to code?+

No. You describe what you want in plain English — Replit Agent writes all the code. Bring a laptop, an idea, and curiosity.

Q.02Do I need a team or an idea to register?+

Neither. Come solo — teams form naturally. No idea yet? You'll find one in the first thirty minutes.

Q.03What do I bring?+

A fully charged laptop. Free Replit credits are provided on the spot. Food, drinks, and the room are included.

Q.04Is there a cost to attend?+

Free. Replit credits and meals included. Each city has its own venue details on Lu.ma — check your city's page.

Q.05What happens to what I build?+

You own everything. Top 3 projects from each city get recognised by Replit and The Product Folks. Several sprint projects have become real startups.

Q.06Who runs each sprint?+

Each city is hosted by its local Product Folks chapter — volunteer builders, founders, and mentors. Replit experts are on-site to help you move faster.
